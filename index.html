IV fluid management during therapeutic hypothermia
💧 Day-by-day fluid rate guide
PhaseRateNotes
Day 1 (0–24h) — Induction40–60 mL/kg/day10% Dextrose; fluid restricted
Day 2 (24–48h) — Late maintenance60–80 mL/kg/dayIncrease if good UO >1 mL/kg/hr; reassess renal function
Day 3 (48–72h) — End phase80–100 mL/kg/dayTitrate to UO, weight, clinical status
Rewarming (~72–78h)100–120 mL/kg/dayTransition to enteral when tolerated; watch for metabolic rebound
⚠️ Target urine output 1–3 mL/kg/hr. Alert senior if UO <1 mL/kg/hr for >2 consecutive hours. Weight gain >10% = overload; weight loss >10% = inadequate intake.

⚡ Calcium action thresholds

iCa 1.0–1.2 mmol/LSupplementAdd Ca gluconate 2 mL/kg/day 10% to maintenance IV; recheck 4–8h

🚫 Calcium IV safety: Never give calcium gluconate via peripheral IV unless absolutely necessary — severe tissue necrosis risk. Use UVC wherever available. Never give as rapid bolus — risk of bradycardia and cardiac arrest. ECG monitoring is mandatory during any IV calcium administration.

🔬 Cranial ultrasound — Resistance Index (RI)

RI = (Peak Systolic Velocity − End Diastolic Velocity) ÷ Peak Systolic Velocity  ·  Anterior cerebral artery

RI valueInterpretationAction
0.60–0.80Normal cerebral perfusionContinue standard monitoring
>0.90Severe cerebral oedemaPoor prognosis — escalate, discuss neurology, consider MRI
<0.55Cerebral hyperaemiaSevere HIE — monitor closely, repeat USS in 12 hours
USS timing: within 24h of admission · repeat day 3–5 · repeat at completion of rewarming.
MRI brain: day 4–5 post-birth (peak DWI sensitivity) — sequences: DWI, T1, T2, MR spectroscopy.
Pharmacological management during therapeutic hypothermia
💊 Dexmedetomidine — sedation and anti-shivering
ParameterDetails
RouteIntravenous continuous infusion
Starting dose0.2 mcg/kg/hour
Maintenance range0.2–0.5 mcg/kg/hour — titrate to effect
Maximum dose0.7 mcg/kg/hour — do not exceed
MonitoringHeart rate + blood pressure every 1 hour
ContraindicationsHR <80 bpm · SBP <40 mmHg · significant AV block
AdvantagesMinimal respiratory depression · combined sedation + analgesia · reduces shivering
⚡ Anticonvulsant ladder — neonatal seizure management
LineDrugLoading doseRouteNotes
1st line Phenobarbitone 20 mg/kg slow IV over 30 min IV Repeat 10 mg/kg if no response. Max cumulative 40 mg/kg.
2nd line Levetiracetam 40–60 mg/kg over 15 min IV Monitor LFTs. Fewer drug interactions. Preferred 2nd agent.
3rd line Midazolam 0.05–0.15 mg/kg/hour infusion IV Respiratory depression risk — continuous SpO₂ monitoring essential.
Alternative Phenytoin 20 mg/kg at max 1 mg/kg/min IV Cardiac monitoring required. Avoid in hepatic failure.
🚫 All anticonvulsant doses require adjustment for renal or hepatic dysfunction during TH. Confirm doses with pharmacy and senior clinician before administration. Document all seizures on seizure chart.
🧪 Magnesium management
Mg levelStatusAction
Mg <0.7 mmol/L Hypomagnesaemia IV MgSO₄ 50%: 0.1 mL/kg (= 0.4 mmol/kg) diluted, given over 30–60 min. Recheck after 4–6 hours.
0.7–1.2 mmol/L Normal Continue routine monitoring; 12-hourly if receiving IV Mg therapy
Mg >2.0 mmol/L Hypermagnesaemia Monitor for respiratory depression, bradycardia, hypotension. Calcium gluconate is antidote — keep at bedside. Allow renal excretion; repeat level in 4–6 hours.
Note: Hypermagnesaemia may occur if maternal MgSO₄ was given antenatally for neuroprotection or preterm labour.

📖 Required documentation — full cooling period
  • Cooling eligibility checklist (signed by specialist)
  • Thompson Score chart — documented at 0, 4, 8, 12, 24, 48, 72 hours
  • Modified Sarnat staging documented at initial assessment
  • aEEG background pattern — documented at 24, 48, 72 hours and each shift handover
  • Cooling observation chart — temperature q15 min during induction, then hourly
  • Fluid balance chart — 4-hourly running total, 24-hour cumulative
  • Seizure chart (commenced at initiation of cooling)
  • Rewarming chart — temperature hourly until 36.5°C

  • Daily senior ward round documentation
